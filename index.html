Computational Biologist · AI for Synthetic Biology

Hi, I’m Evangelos-Marios Nikolados, a Ph.D. researcher at the University of Edinburgh working at the intersection of computational biology, synthetic biology, and machine learning.

My research focuses on developing mechanistic and data-driven models for optimizing protein expression and understanding cellular growth dynamics. I integrate dynamical systems, host–circuit modeling, and deep learning to uncover how biological networks behave under engineered perturbations.

I previously studied at Imperial College London, Harvard University, and Columbia University, and have worked as a Research Assistant in the Weiße Group at the University of Edinburgh.
